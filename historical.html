Zahir Mosque

Operating Hour: Everyday
Entrance Fee: Free
Address: Alor Setar,Kedah

Open in 1912. Kedah’s National Mosque served as Alor Setar’s central hub for local Muslims. It is also one of the grandest and oldest mosques in Malaysia. Most surprisingly, it voted as one of Top 10 of most beautiful mosques in the world. Zahir Mosque was built upon the tomb and grave of fallen Kedah warrior that die during Siamese invasion of Kedah. The state’s annual Quran-reading competition is held within the premise of the mosque. It also exhibits a Moorish design with five black dimes on the western flank of Dataran Alor Setar. Kedah’s whitewashed State Mosque boasts impressive decorations and artistic Arabic inscriptions on the walls, just above the entrance, blue glass mosaics form intricate patterns.


Penang War Museum

Operating Hour: Everyday (9.00am – 6.00pm) they also do night tours
Entrance Fee: - Malaysians - Adult RM 15 ǀ Child (5 - 12 years) RM 7.50
- Foreigners / Tourists - Adult RM 30 ǀ Child (5 - 12 years) RM 15
- Foreigners with a work permit or Malaysian permanent resident can pay the local prices if they show a valid ID.
Address: Bukit Maung, Penang

The Penang War Museum is located on top of Bukit Maung. It was built to commemorate the original defence complex built by the British before World War II where it became a defensive fort during the war. Today, it is a historical complex that showcases war equipment and shows historical timelines. This war museum is a dedication to those who have served and sacrificed, defending the country. If you are here, we recommend that you dress brightly and comfortably, wear not too thick clothes, wear comfortable shoes, wear a dark coloured hat, bring binoculars, insect repellent spray, notebooks, and pens.

Kelly’s Castle

Operating Hour: Everyday (9.00am – 6.00pm)
Entrance Fee: - RM 5 (foreigner)
- RM 4 (adult)
- RM 3 (secondary school students)
- RM 2 (primary school students)
- FREE (child aged six and below)
Address: Kellie’s Castle, 31000 Batu Gajah

Kellie’s Castle is a historic building built by Britain’s leading rubber farmer, William Kellie Smith in 1910. He bought a 960 acres plot of land to be used as a rubber plantation and site for building houses. However, the construction of the palace had to be stopped immediately because Kellie died suddenly in 1926. Although not fully completed, the palace is said to have a lot of secret space including underground tunnels. This three-story building was inspired by the design of the Moghul palace in Agra, India and its wall tiles were imported from Italy. The bricks are glued using a mixture of duck egg white, sand, lime, brown sugar and honey, no iron or nails are used.


The Duyong Fort

Operating Hour: Everyday (8.00am – 6.00pm) ǀ Friday Close
Entrance Fee: Free
Address: Pulau Duyong Kecil, 20100 Kuala Terengganu

Located just 7 km from the city of Kuala Terengganu on Pulau Duyong, Kota Lama Duyong was built in 1919 by a senior official of the Terengganu State Administration, Dato 'Biji Sura. This house is called the Old City because it is also surrounded by stone walls. What is interesting about this house, it is a house built using bricks that was the only one found in this area at that time. The design is inspired by the architecture of Malay Terengganu during the 1920s.

Kota A’Famosa

Operating Hour: Everyday
Entrance Fee: Free
Address: Jalan Parameswara, Bandar Hilir, 78000 Alor Gajah, Melaka

Kota A’Famosa was built in 1511 and is the most popular landmark in Malacca. According to history it was established by the Portuguese colonialists as their stronghold in Malacca. The 500-year-old building has a gate also known as the Santiago Gate. This gate is the oldest architectural structure of Europe and remains in Asia. Kota A’Famosa located in Bandar Hilir is a must visit if you are on holiday to Malacca because it is part of the history of the country and the tourist landmarks of the state.


Agnes Keith House

Operating Hour: Everyday (9:00a.m – 5.00pm)
Entrance Fee: Older RM2 ǀ Senior Citizens over 55 years & Children under 12 years old Free
Address: Agnes Keith House, 90000 Sandakan, Sabah

The house, also called Newlands, was once inhabited by an American-born writer, Agnes Keith in 1939, best known for her book Land Below the Wind. The work of the late has made Sabah famous to this day where his book clearly tells about the life of the local indigenous people of Sabah and the beauty of its nature during the British rule at that time. After that, Agnes wrote two more books entitled Three Came Home in 1946 and White Man Returns in 1951. The books were written in the reading room of the house which is located on the hill of the city center from which can be seen directly the beauty of Sandakan Bay and the Sea Sulu.


Margherita Fort

Operating Hour: Everyday (9:00a.m – 4.45pm)
Entrance Fee: Free
Address: Petra Jaya, 93050 Kuching, Sarawak

This white fort was completed with the design of an old English palace in 1879 by Sir Charles Brooke and was named after his wife, Margaret Brooke. The fort was built to symbolize the stability of the government and its strength. Located on a hill, this fort offers sweeping views in all directions in the surrounding area. This fort is the most significant monument to the city of Kuching in terms of history, design, and tourism.
