With its cool air, lush hillscape and touch of colonial nostalgia, Cameron Highlands lures both locals and globetrotters to this weekend wonderland. Here is a comprehensive list of things to do in the highlands, from the not-to-be-missed activities, to uniquely local favorites. Keep this for your next trip!


Colmare Tropicale


To start your adventure in Bukit Tinggi we recommend visiting Colmar Tropicale French Village first. Opened by Berjaya Hills Resort, this French-themed village is inspired by Colmar Village Alsace, a French village famous for its cobbled streets and Renaissance relics. The atmosphere of this village will make anyone who walks here feel as if they are in a classic European film

Apart from the unique atmosphere, there are also many exciting activities that await you and your family such as rock climbing and mountain biking. For a complete holiday experience in Bukit Tinggi, you can choose to spend the night at the resort. For those who plan to return for the day, a small fee is charged to visit the area. Entrance Fee: Adult (RM16) | Children (RM8)


Japanese Village

Without boarding a plane, in just a few minutes you can follow from a romantic French village to a peaceful traditional Japanese village. The Japanese Village located in Bukit Tinggi is a collection of Japanese themed attractions that are neatly arranged around a large green park with the aim of reviving the atmosphere of the traditional Japanese village in the past.

This Japanese village is decorated with beautiful Koi fish ponds, flower gardens and mossy rocks. Family-friendly attractions that can be found here are Japanese Tea House, Botanical Garden, Tatami Spa, Ume Tatami Suite and Ryo Zan Tei restaurant. Entrance Fee: Adult (RM12) | Children (RM8)


Botanical Garden

If you are strolling in the panoramic expansive flower garden, the Bukit Tinggi Botanical Garden awaits your presence. Just 5 minutes drive from Colmar Tropicale, a hidden park within the Japanese Village, it has an area of ​​4 acres and features a variety of cool climate plants such as Strawberry and Pear trees

A special walkway 1 kilometer through the park and makes this place ideal for a leisurely walk with family or loved ones while enjoying the variety of flora and fauna that adorn this park


Tatami Spa

Recognized as the first Japanese Spa-concept outside Japan. The spa, which was awarded the best spa in 2007-2008 by the Hospitality Asia Platinum Awards, offers a variety of special body treatments such as scrubs, therapeutic hot tubs, aromatherapy massages and a special body treatment called Kimochi Treatment. In addition, you can choose to relax in two separate hot tubs, the first is the closed tub inside and the second is the Onsen tab located on the outside
